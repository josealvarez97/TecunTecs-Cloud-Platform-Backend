Overview of Navier-Stokes Equations​

A partial differential equation is an equation of the form:

f(u,∂u∂x,...,∂xnu,∂u∂y,...,∂ynu,x,y)=0f(u, \frac{\partial u}{\partial x}, ..., \partial^n_x u, \frac{\partial u}{\partial y}, ..., \partial^n_y u, x, y)=0f(u,∂x∂u​,...,∂xn​u,∂y∂u​,...,∂yn​u,x,y)=0

It is important to remember that there is nothing different about the Navier-Stokes equations, the mathematical model that governs the dynamics of fluids.

Continuity equation (conservation of mass)​

∇⋅u=0\nabla \cdot \mathbf{u}=0∇⋅u=0
⇕\Updownarrow⇕
∂u∂x+∂u∂y=0\frac{\partial u}{\partial x} + \frac{\partial u}{\partial y} = 0∂x∂u​+∂y∂u​=0

Conservation (balance) of momentum​

∂u∂t+u⋅∇u=−1ρ∇p+ν∇2u\frac{\partial \mathbf{u}}{\partial t} + \mathbf{u}\cdot \nabla \mathbf{u} = - \frac{1}{\rho} \nabla p + \nu \nabla^2\mathbf{u}∂t∂u​+u⋅∇u=−ρ1​∇p+ν∇2u
⇕\Updownarrow⇕
∂u∂t+(u∂u∂x+v∂u∂y)=−1ρ∂p∂x+ν(∂2u∂2x+∂2u∂2y)\frac{\partial u}{\partial t} + \left( u \frac{\partial u}{\partial x}+ v\frac{\partial u}{\partial y} \right) = - \frac{1}{\rho} \frac{\partial p}{\partial x} + \nu \left(\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial^2 x} + \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial^2 y}\right)∂t∂u​+(u∂x∂u​+v∂y∂u​)=−ρ1​∂x∂p​+ν(∂2x∂2u​+∂2y∂2u​)
∂v∂t+(u∂v∂x+v∂v∂y)=−1ρ∂p∂y+ν(∂2v∂2x+∂2v∂2y)\frac{\partial v}{\partial t} + \left( u \frac{\partial v}{\partial x}+ v\frac{\partial v}{\partial y} \right) = - \frac{1}{\rho} \frac{\partial p}{\partial y} + \nu \left(\frac{\partial^2 v}{\partial^2 x} + \frac{\partial^2 v}{\partial^2 y}\right)∂t∂v​+(u∂x∂v​+v∂y∂v​)=−ρ1​∂y∂p​+ν(∂2x∂2v​+∂2y∂2v​)
